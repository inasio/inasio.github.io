History will not be kind

Posted on 2018.03.08 · Tagged in books, movies

One of the classic tropes in books and movies (I guess I should say stories) is the one, the hero that shows up to save the day, starts up weak and ignorant, and grows to be wise and strong. Quite a few of these heros were, unknown to them at first, descendants of similarly heroic people. It is then most natural that they grow to be leaders and saviors, perhaps their special heritage/blood comes with cool powers that eventually manifest themselves. The jedi, Targaryens, Aragorns, Potters, etc. This is certainly a powerful trope, present in some of the most succesful media properties.

How is this different from saying: you know your history, no heroic blood back there, hence just sit tight and let the special people do the heavy lifting, the guiding, the deciding. You get to be a grunt and that’s as far as you are going to go. This is the royal blood concept all over our culture, permeating the world and influencing it. I think there is a counter-current that is starting to catch up on this, I wouldn’t be surprised if this is why Disney decided to make (or at least pretend) Rey in new Star Wars movies come from a non-force backgound…
