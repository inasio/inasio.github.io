Read All the Books

Posted on 2017.10.05 · Tagged in books

I like to read books. The first real books that I ever read, just lines of words in each page, were the Sandokan books by Emilio Salgari (Salgari is probably worth writing a post or two, at the very least). I read that I could find, then read them again, and then I read them again. I remember being frustrated that by chance I had managed to luck out and start reading with world’s very best most excellent books, the frustration being that it was all going to be downhill from here. I mentioned this to my uncle Rafa, my go-to guru for all things book-related, I don’t remember if he laughed or sighed or just tilted his head in a funny way. We were in his house where practically every single wall is covered in shelves and books, he must have looked around, and then produced a stack of books that he said would maybe come a bit close to the incredible heights of the adventures of Sandokan and his pals Tremal Naik, Kammamuri, etc.

I kept on reading lots, read a lot of books while in school, while my parents were driving, snuck under the sheets. I eventually got over Salgari and read basically anything I could find, including the monthly (weekly?) Readers Digest cover to cover, and even browsed the phone book in a few desperate times.

Fast-forward a few years (this feels like such a dated post, phone books, fast-forwarding, Readers Digest…), google was the new thing in town, I no longer had to go through Altavista, Yahoo, or even dogpile (a metasearch engine from way back). Suddenly google was sexy, had a bunch of cash and cool projects. The one that really blew my mind was google books, their plan to scan every single book. I had a revelation back then: I wanted to work for them, as my ultimate goal in life was/is/should-be to read all the books. Not necessarily meaning that it would be me reading all the books, I’m not stupid, some of those Readers Digest were pretty bad, not keen on going there again. Maybe later I’ll write about how to go about it…
